# Hypertrophy Science & Training Principles

## The Three Mechanisms of Hypertrophy

1. **Mechanical tension** -- the primary driver. Heavy loads through a full range of motion create the stimulus for muscle growth. This is why progressive overload matters more than anything else.
2. **Metabolic stress** -- the "pump." Accumulation of metabolites (lactate, hydrogen ions) during higher-rep sets contributes to growth, especially for type I fibers.
3. **Muscle damage** -- controlled micro-trauma from eccentric loading and novel stimuli. Useful but not something to chase; excessive damage impairs recovery without additional growth.

**Practical implication**: train primarily in the 6-12 rep range for mechanical tension with metabolic stress, periodically include 12-20 rep work for metabolic emphasis, and use 3-6 rep heavy work for neural adaptations and strength peaks.

## Volume Landmarks (per Dr. Mike Israetel / Renaissance Periodization)

These are weekly SET counts per muscle group for an intermediate lifter. Verify and adjust based on the user's actual recovery and progress data.

| Muscle Group | MEV (Minimum Effective Volume) | MAV (Maximum Adaptive Volume) | MRV (Maximum Recoverable Volume) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chest | 8 sets | 12-16 sets | 20-22 sets |
| Back (width) | 8 sets | 12-16 sets | 20-22 sets |
| Back (thickness) | 6 sets | 10-14 sets | 18-20 sets |
| Shoulders (side delts) | 8 sets | 14-18 sets | 22-26 sets |
| Shoulders (rear delts) | 6 sets | 12-16 sets | 20-22 sets |
| Biceps | 6 sets | 10-14 sets | 18-20 sets |
| Triceps | 6 sets | 8-12 sets | 16-18 sets |
| Quads | 6 sets | 10-14 sets | 18-20 sets |
| Hamstrings | 6 sets | 8-12 sets | 16-18 sets |
| Glutes | 4 sets | 8-12 sets | 16-18 sets |
| Calves | 8 sets | 12-16 sets | 20 sets |
| Abs | 0 sets (compound) | 6-10 sets | 16 sets |

**How to use these**:
- Start a mesocycle near MEV
- Add 1-2 sets per muscle group per week (progressive volume overload)
- When approaching MRV, fatigue accumulates -- this is when you deload
- After deload, restart at MEV with higher loads (you're now stronger)

## Progressive Overload Methods

Ranked by priority. Use the highest-priority method that's available:

| Priority | Method | How | When |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Load increase** | Add weight to the bar/machine | When all prescribed reps are hit with good form for all sets |
| 2 | **Rep increase** | Add reps within the prescribed range | When load can't increase (micro-plates unavailable, or near top of range) |
| 3 | **Set increase** | Add a set to the exercise | When near top of rep range and load is stuck |
| 4 | **Tempo manipulation** | Slow the eccentric (3-4s) | When other methods are exhausted or for technique phases |
| 5 | **ROM increase** | Use a fuller range of motion (deficit, paused reps) | Plateau buster, periodization variety |
| 6 | **Exercise variation** | Swap to a harder variation of the same pattern | When a movement is fully milked and adaptation has plateaued |

### Minimum Load Increments

| Equipment | Increment |
|---|---|
| Barbell (standard plates) | 2.5kg (1.25kg per side) |
| Barbell (micro plates) | 1kg (0.5kg per side) |
| Dumbbells | 2kg (next dumbbell up) |
| Cable machines | 1 plate/pin (varies, usually 2.5-5kg) |
| Bodyweight | Add reps, slow tempo, or add external load |

## Periodization Models

### Linear Periodization (Beginner-Intermediate)

```
Week 1-4: Accumulation (moderate load, higher reps 10-12, volume increasing)
Week 5-8: Intensification (higher load, moderate reps 6-8, volume maintained)
Week 9: Deload (50-60% volume, 50-70% load)
Repeat with higher baseline loads
```

### Undulating Periodization (Intermediate-Advanced)

Vary rep ranges across the week:

| Day | Focus | Reps | PSE |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 1 (Heavy) | Strength-hypertrophy | 4-6 | 8-9 |
| Day 2 (Moderate) | Classic hypertrophy | 8-12 | 7-8 |
| Day 3 (Light) | Metabolic / pump | 12-20 | 6-7 |

### Block Periodization (Advanced)

3-4 week blocks with specific emphasis:

| Block | Focus | Volume | Intensity | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Anatomical adaptation | Work capacity, technique | High | Low-moderate | 2-3 weeks |
| Hypertrophy accumulation | Muscle growth | High, increasing | Moderate | 4-6 weeks |
| Strength intensification | Neural adaptations | Moderate, decreasing | High | 3-4 weeks |
| Deload / pivot | Recovery, resensitization | Low | Low | 1 week |

## Rep Range Optimization by Muscle Group

Not all muscles respond identically. Research-backed rep range biases:

| Muscle Group | Optimal Primary Range | Secondary Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chest | 8-12 | 6-8 (strength), 12-15 (pump) | Responds well to stretch-mediated loading |
| Back (lats) | 8-12 | 12-15 (mind-muscle connection) | Prioritize full stretch at bottom |
| Quads | 8-12 | 12-20 (leg extensions, high-rep squats) | Tolerates higher reps well |
| Hamstrings | 8-12 | 6-8 (RDL/stiff-leg), 12-15 (curls) | Respond to both heavy and moderate |
| Side delts | 12-20 | 8-12 (heavier laterals) | High volume tolerant, recover fast |
| Rear delts | 12-20 | 15-25 (face pulls, reverse flyes) | Similar to side delts |
| Biceps | 8-12 | 12-15 (cables, high-rep finishers) | Prone to tendon irritation if too heavy |
| Triceps | 8-12 | 6-8 (close grip bench/dips), 12-15 (pushdowns) | Long head needs overhead work |
| Calves | 10-15 | 15-25 (seated calf raise) | Need high volume, slow eccentrics |
| Abs | 10-15 | 15-25 (stability work) | Progressive overload still applies |

## Training Frequency by Muscle Group

For hypertrophy, each muscle should be trained **2x per week minimum** to maximize muscle protein synthesis windows (~48-72 hours per stimulus).

| Frequency | Best For | Split Examples |
|---|---|---|
| 2x/week | Most muscle groups, most lifters | Upper/Lower, Push/Pull/Legs (6 day) |
| 3x/week | Lagging muscle groups, small muscles | Full body 3x, or adding extra sessions |
| 1x/week | Only acceptable for very high per-session volume | Bro split (suboptimal for natural lifters) |

## Rest Periods

From the user's program, rest periods are prescribed per exercise. The general guidelines:

| Rep Range | Rest Period | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| 1-5 reps (strength) | 3-5 minutes | Full ATP/CP recovery for max force |
| 6-8 reps (strength-hypertrophy) | 2-3 minutes | Near-full recovery |
| 8-12 reps (hypertrophy) | 90-120 seconds | Balance recovery and metabolic stress |
| 12-20 reps (metabolic) | 60-90 seconds | Accumulate metabolites |
| 20+ reps (endurance/pump) | 30-60 seconds | Maximum metabolic stress |

**If the user can't hit the same reps with the same weight on the next set, rest longer.** Preserving performance trumps arbitrary rest timers.

## Warm-Up Protocol (from the program)

Per the user's PT instructions:

1. **Mobility work** first (5-10 min of dynamic stretching targeting the session's muscle groups)
2. **2 warm-up sets per muscle group** for the first exercise of each group:
   - Set 1: 15-20 reps at ~40-50% working weight
   - Set 2: 12-15 reps at ~60-70% working weight
3. Warm-up sets are **NOT to failure** -- the goal is to prime the movement pattern and increase local blood flow
4. No warm-up needed for subsequent exercises targeting the same muscle group

## Bracing Technique (from the program)

Critical for compound lifts (squat, deadlift, stiff-leg deadlift):

1. Take a full breath in, then exhale forcefully -- contract the rib cage area
2. Keep the area compressed and draw the navel toward the spine
3. Maintain this brace throughout the entire movement while breathing normally
4. Re-brace at the top of each rep for heavy sets

## Tracking What Matters

Minimum data to log every session:
- Exercise name
- Sets completed
- Reps per set
- Load per set (in kg)
- PSE for the session (overall)
- Any notes (form issues, pain, energy level)

Optional but valuable:
- Body weight (daily, fasted, for trend tracking)
- Sleep hours and quality (1-5 scale)
- Session duration
- Specific PSE per exercise (not just session-wide)
